Hazur Sahib (Sachkhand Sri Hazur Abchalnagar Sahib)

Takht Sri Hazur Sahib, officially Takht Sachkhand Sri Hazur Abchalnagar Sahib, is one of the five revered Takhts in Sikhism, located in Nanded, Maharashtra, on the banks of the Godavari River Golden Temple Amritsar . Built between 1832 and 1837 under the patronage of Maharaja Ranjit Singh, this majestic gurdwara stands at the historic site where Guru Gobind Singh Ji, the tenth Sikh Guru, lived and eventually breathed his last in 1708 The architectural style merges Sikh and Mughal influences, featuring white marble walls adorned with intricate floral inlays, stucco and tukari work, and a stunning gold-plated ribbed dome topped with a gilded copper kalash finial . The inner sanctum, known as Angitha Sahib, is built directly over the site of the Guru’s cremation and guarded as a sacred space. It houses priceless relics left by Guru Gobind Singh Ji, including his golden dagger, matchlock gun, quiver of 35 arrows, steel shield with gemstones, and five golden swords During the day, the Guru Granth Sahib, the eternal scripture, is placed in front of the sanctum; at night, it is returned to its resting place inside the sanctum Incredible India . Hazur Sahib is also the site where the Guru formally declared the Guru Granth Sahib as the eternal Guru, naming Nanded as “Abchalnagar” (the Steadfast City) Alamy .

Mahur Temple (Renuka Devi Temple)

Renuka Devi Temple is a revered Hindu pilgrimage site located in Mahur, Nanded district, Maharashtra. Perched atop a hill surrounded by lush green forests, this ancient temple is dedicated to Goddess Renuka, the mother of Lord Parashurama, the sixth incarnation of Lord Vishnu. It is considered one of the 51 Shaktipeethas, making it a significant spiritual destination, especially for devotees of the Shakti tradition. Legend says that Renuka Devi was a virtuous and powerful woman, and after her death, Lord Parashurama built this temple to honor her. Pilgrims climb around 400 steps to reach the temple, enjoying breathtaking views along the way. The temple is particularly crowded during Navratri, when thousands of devotees visit for darshan and special rituals. The peaceful surroundings and sacred atmosphere make Mahur not only a religious hub but also a serene retreat. Besides the Renuka Devi Temple, Mahur also houses temples of Anusaya Mata and Dattatreya, adding to its spiritual significance. The temple is managed by the Maharashtra government and local trust. Visiting Mahur offers both spiritual upliftment and a deep connection with ancient Indian culture and mythology. It is a must-visit for anyone exploring Maharashtra’s rich heritage.

Kandhar Fort

Kandhar Fort, located in Kandhar town of Nanded district, Maharashtra, is a historically significant hill fort known for its rich past and impressive architecture. Built around the 10th century during the reign of the Rashtrakuta dynasty, this fort was an important military stronghold in the Deccan region. Its strategic location and robust construction made it a prized possession for various dynasties including the Chalukyas, Bahmanis, Mughals, and Marathas. The fort is surrounded by a deep moat and strong stone walls, which are still partially intact today. Inside the fort, one can find ruins of old structures like watchtowers, wells, temples, and barracks. The architecture showcases a blend of Hindu and Islamic influences, reflecting the changing rulers over centuries. The fort offers panoramic views of the surrounding countryside and attracts history enthusiasts, photographers, and trekkers alike. Kandhar Fort is also famous for its connection to the legendary Rashtrakuta king Krishna III, who is believed to have contributed to its development. Today, although in a ruined state, the fort stands as a silent testimony to the valor, culture, and architectural prowess of medieval Maharashtra. It is a must-visit heritage site for those exploring the historical gems of the Marathwada region.

Raher Temple

Raher Temple, dedicated to Goddess Renuka Mata, is a revered pilgrimage site located near Mudkhed in Nanded district, Maharashtra. This ancient temple holds great spiritual significance for devotees of Renuka Devi, who is worshipped as the mother of Lord Parashurama—one of the ten avatars of Lord Vishnu. The temple is nestled in a serene rural setting surrounded by greenery and farmlands, making it a peaceful retreat for worshippers. The idol of Renuka Mata in the Raher temple is beautifully adorned with traditional ornaments, colorful clothes, and flowers. The temple architecture is modest yet sacred, reflecting the faith and devotion of the local community. Every year, large numbers of devotees visit Raher, especially during Navratri and Dasara festivals, to seek the blessings of the Goddess. Special rituals, bhajans, and cultural programs are organized during this period. The temple also serves as a hub of local cultural identity, where age-old Marathi traditions are celebrated with enthusiasm. Pilgrims often combine their visit to Raher with nearby temples in Mahur and Nanded. The divine atmosphere and the spiritual energy of Raher Temple make it a significant religious destination in the Marathwada region.
